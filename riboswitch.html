Riboswitches

In molecular biology, a riboswitch is a regulatory segment of a messenger RNA (mRNA) molecule that is affected by a trigger of some kind, and helps to control translation.
Translation Translation involves “decoding” a messenger RNA (mRNA) and using its information to build a protein, or chain of amino acids.
Function of a Riboswitch
In the off state the RBS is occluded due to the formation of a loop structure that does not allow translation to take place. However when it is triggered(on state), the loop melts and exposes the RBS which enables the binding of the ribosome to the RBS and hence results in translation or protein expression.
